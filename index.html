Beneficial Ingredients

A – Z
A
Azelaic Acid
found in: Anua serum
anti-acne anti-inflammatory brightening
A naturally occurring acid found in grains. One of the most uniquely suited ingredients for your specific situation.
Kills P. acnes bacteria · Inhibits 5-alpha-reductase (the enzyme that converts testosterone to DHT, the androgen driving your hormonal acne) · Directly calms inflammatory pathways · Fades post-acne hyperpigmentation · Mild exfoliant · Works through two separate anti-inflammatory mechanisms simultaneously
Works synergistically with zinc and vitamin B6 for DHT inhibition
Adenosine
found in: Anua serum
anti-aging anti-inflammatory
A naturally occurring molecule present in all living cells.
Supports collagen synthesis · Anti-inflammatory properties · Helps with wound healing · Improves skin elasticity over time
Aloe Barbadensis (Aloe Vera)
found in: Cetaphil SPF, Rejuran
anti-inflammatory hydration
Plant extract with a long history of use for soothing skin.
Calming and soothing · Hydrating · Mildly anti-inflammatory · Good addition in SPF for your inflamed skin
Alpha-Bisabolol
also: Bisabolol · found in: Rejuran, CeraVe PM
anti-inflammatory
The isolated, purified active molecule extracted from chamomile. More potent and consistent than whole chamomile extract because you get a precise concentration of just the active compound.

B
Benzoyl Peroxide
found in: Neutrogena On-The-Spot 2.5%
anti-acne
An oxidizing agent used as a spot treatment. One of the most evidence-backed topical acne treatments available.
Bactericidal — kills P. acnes by flooding the pore with oxygen, which is toxic to the anaerobic bacteria · Does not cause antibiotic resistance (unlike topical antibiotics) · 2.5% is as effective as 10% with significantly less irritation · When combined with blue LED therapy, reduces inflammatory lesions by up to 79% in studies
Photosensitive to UV light — use at night only. Apply on bare skin before other products.
C
Barrier Trio — these three work together to rebuild your skin barrier. Ceramides are the structure, cholesterol is the mortar, phytosphingosine is the raw material your skin uses to make more ceramides.
Ceramides (NP, AP, EOP)
found in: CeraVe PM, Amazon Retinol Serum, CeraVe Body Lotion, CeraVe Body Wash
barrier
Lipid molecules that make up approximately 50% of the mortar between your skin cells. Your skin barrier is structured like a brick wall — ceramides are the mortar.
Directly replenishes the lipids your barrier is literally made of · Prevents moisture loss · Keeps irritants and bacteria out · Three types (NP, AP, EOP) work together for complete barrier repair · Essential for compromised or inflamed skin
Require cholesterol and phytosphingosine to function optimally
Cholesterol
found in: CeraVe PM, Amazon Retinol Serum, CeraVe Body Wash
barrier
A lipid that forms part of your skin barrier's structural mortar alongside ceramides and fatty acids.
Works synergistically with ceramides — without cholesterol the barrier structure doesn't hold properly regardless of ceramide levels · Makes up roughly 25% of the skin barrier lipid ratio · Essential structural partner to ceramides
Part of the barrier trio with ceramides and phytosphingosine
Phytosphingosine
found in: CeraVe PM, Amazon Retinol Serum, CeraVe Body Wash
barrier
A precursor molecule — meaning your skin uses it as raw material to manufacture its own ceramides internally.
Enables your skin to build ceramides from scratch rather than just receiving them topically · More bioavailable than ceramides alone · Supports long-term barrier self-repair · Antimicrobial properties
Part of the barrier trio with ceramides and cholesterol
Centella Asiatica Family — a plant extract and its isolated active compounds. Found across multiple products in your routine. Each form has different potency and specificity.
Centella Asiatica Extract
also: Gotu Kola · found in: Nécessaire Body Wash, Drunk Elephant Sili Whipped, Cetaphil Firming Lotion, Cicaplast Balm
anti-inflammatory barrier anti-aging
A whole plant extract from Centella Asiatica, a herb used in traditional Asian medicine for centuries. Contains multiple active triterpenoid compounds including madecassoside, asiaticoside, asiatic acid, and madecassic acid.
Stimulates collagen synthesis · Directly suppresses inflammatory cytokines · Accelerates wound healing · Strengthens skin barrier · Improves skin texture over time · Well studied in both traditional and modern dermatological research · Works on inflammation through different pathways than niacinamide making them complementary when used together
Madecassoside
isolated active from Centella Asiatica · found in: Cicaplast Balm B5+
anti-inflammatory barrier anti-aging
The most studied and potent isolated active compound from Centella Asiatica. The "cica" in Cicaplast refers to cicatrization — the French medical term for wound healing — built around this ingredient.
More potent and consistent than whole centella extract — isolated form means precise concentration · Strongly promotes wound healing and tissue repair · Significant anti-inflammatory activity · Stimulates collagen and elastin production · Helps repair damaged skin barrier · Particularly useful post-procedure or on compromised skin
Works alongside asiaticoside and asiatic acid in the centella family
Asiaticoside / Asiatic Acid
additional actives from Centella Asiatica
anti-inflammatory anti-aging
Two other key triterpenoid compounds found in centella asiatica alongside madecassoside. Work synergistically with it rather than as standalone ingredients.
Collagen synthesis stimulation · Anti-inflammatory · Wound healing support · Antioxidant properties · Typically present when whole centella extract is listed rather than isolated madecassoside
G
Glycerin
found in: CeraVe PM, Rejuran, CeraVe Body Lotion
hydration
A humectant — draws moisture from the surrounding environment into your skin.
Highly effective hydration · Works especially well in humid climates like Florida where there's abundant atmospheric moisture to draw from · Non-comedogenic · Well tolerated by all skin types · Very well studied
H
Hyaluronic Acid
also: Sodium Hyaluronate · found in: Anua serum, CeraVe PM, Rejuran, CeraVe Body Wash
hydration
A naturally occurring molecule already present in your skin and connective tissue. A humectant that holds up to 1000x its weight in water.
Draws moisture into skin · Works especially well in humid environments — Florida's humidity means it always has atmospheric moisture to pull from · Plumps the appearance of skin · Non-comedogenic · Sodium hyaluronate is a smaller molecular form that penetrates more easily
J

INCI: Simmondsia Chinensis Seed Oil · found in: Rejuran
barrier hydration

Mimics sebum so skin recognizes it easily · Non-comedogenic · Can signal skin to reduce its own sebum production · Barrier supportive
In combination with other heavy oils in Rejuran, may be too occlusive for your oily acne-prone skin type.
L
Lactic Acid
not currently in your routine — for future consideration
anti-aging hydration
An AHA (alpha hydroxy acid) exfoliant, but uniquely also a humectant. Larger molecule than glycolic acid so penetrates more slowly and gently.
Gently exfoliates dead skin cells · Simultaneously hydrates while exfoliating — unlike other AHAs · Less irritating than glycolic acid · Best exfoliant option for sensitive or inflamed skin when you're ready to reintroduce exfoliation · Helps with cell turnover and brightening
Hold off until skin is more stable. Reintroduce at low percentage once weekly.

GHK-Cu (Copper Peptide)
Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine Copper · prescribed injectable
anti-aging barrier anti-inflammatory
A naturally occurring copper-binding peptide that declines significantly with age. Prescribed to you as a daily injectable, delivering it systemically at concentrations topical application cannot match.
Stimulates collagen and elastin synthesis systemically · Accelerates wound healing and tissue repair · Anti-inflammatory effects throughout the body · Supports skin barrier repair · Antioxidant properties · Injectable form bypasses skin barrier entirely — acts at a fundamentally different level than topical peptides · Works synergistically with your topical retinol — retinol stimulates collagen locally while GHK-Cu works systemically · Reduces urgency of needing retinol specifically in body lotion since collagen support is already addressed from inside

anti-aging barrier
DNA fragments derived from salmon sperm. Has clinical backing for wound healing and tissue repair, used in injectable treatments in Korea before being adopted in topicals.
Tissue repair · Barrier support · Collagen stimulation · Anti-inflammatory · Particularly beneficial post-procedure (explains why Rejuran is popular post-microneedling)
Rejuran's heavy oil base makes it less ideal for your oily acne-prone skin despite this ingredient being beneficial.
R
Retinoid Family — all vitamin A derivatives. All ultimately work by binding to retinoic acid receptors in skin cells. Differ only in how many conversion steps are needed before becoming active. Fewer steps = faster acting = more potent = more irritating.
Retinol
found in: Amazon Retinol Serum, ZO Eye Cream
anti-aging anti-acne
Over-the-counter vitamin A derivative. Requires two enzymatic conversion steps before becoming active retinoic acid: retinol → retinaldehyde → retinoic acid.
Accelerates cell turnover · Stimulates collagen production in dermis · Reduces sebaceous gland activity · Fades hyperpigmentation · Reduces acne · Results appear at 3-6 months, best results at 6-12 months · More gradual and gentle than tretinoin due to conversion process · Use at night only — photosensitive
Tretinoin is the fully active form — same destination, faster path
Tretinoin
Retinoic acid · prescription only
anti-aging anti-acne
Prescription retinoid. Already in its fully active retinoic acid form — zero conversion steps needed. Binds to receptors immediately on contact.
Same benefits as retinol but significantly faster and more potent · Produces equivalent anti-aging results in approximately one third the time · More powerful sebum suppression — better for hormonal acne specifically · Gold standard for both acne and anti-aging · You've used it before with good results
Purging phase (skin looks worse before better) lasts 4-8 weeks. Worth discussing with your endocrinologist.
Retinaldehyde
Retinal · OTC, less common
anti-aging anti-acne
One conversion step from active retinoic acid. Middle ground between retinol and tretinoin, still available over the counter.
Significantly more potent than retinol · More gentle than tretinoin · Good option if retinol feels insufficient but tretinoin isn't accessible · Less common in products

Vitamin E in topical form. Tocopherol is the pure form; tocopheryl acetate is the more stable ester form. Both are antioxidants.
Neutralizes free radicals before they can break down collagen · Protects against oxidative damage from UV, pollution, and blue light · Helps stabilize other antioxidants · Tocopherol is more immediately active; tocopheryl acetate is more stable in formula · Oral vitamin E also contributes to skin health systemically

not currently in your routine

Functional-Only Ingredients

no skin benefit

These ingredients perform necessary roles in product formulation — preserving, emulsifying, thickening, or stabilizing — but provide no direct benefit to your skin. They are not harmful (with exceptions noted), just functionally neutral from a skincare standpoint.

Behentrimonium Methosulfate
Emulsifier — helps oil and water mix together in a formula. Common in CeraVe products. No skin benefit, just keeps the product stable and consistent in texture.
Carbomer
Thickening agent — creates gel-like texture. Found in many of your products. Purely a texture and consistency agent. No skin benefit.
Caprylyl Glycol
Preservative booster — extends the effectiveness of other preservatives. Also has very mild moisturizing properties but not at concentrations used. Primarily functional.
Cetearyl Alcohol / Cetyl Alcohol
Fatty alcohols — despite the name, these are not drying like rubbing alcohol. They are emollients and emulsifiers that give creams a smooth texture. Not the same category as irritating alcohols. No active skin benefit.
Dimethicone
Silicone — creates a smooth, slip-like feel on skin and forms a light occlusive barrier to seal moisture in. Not harmful and mildly beneficial in barrier sealing, but primarily a texture and feel agent. Can feel heavy for some oily skin types.
Ethylhexylglycerin
Preservative booster with mild skin conditioning properties — helps other preservatives work more effectively and improves product spreadability. Considered safe and gentle. Functionally neutral from a treatment standpoint.
Fragrance / Parfum
The most common hidden irritant in skincare. Serves no functional benefit to your skin — purely aesthetic. A single "fragrance" listing can represent dozens of undisclosed chemical compounds. Particularly problematic for inflamed, sensitive, or acne-prone skin. Avoid wherever possible — this is a genuine recommendation, not just caution.
Glyceryl Stearate
Emulsifier — helps bind oil and water phases of a formula. No skin benefit. Very commonly used and well tolerated.
Methylparaben / Propylparaben
Preservatives from the paraben family. Considered safe at cosmetic concentrations by regulatory standards. However they are weak estrogen mimickers (endocrine disruptors) at high doses. Given your active hormonal investigation, minimizing additional potential endocrine-disrupting inputs is a reasonable precaution even if the actual risk at cosmetic concentrations is low. Found in CeraVe Daily Moisturizing Lotion (body) — the Soothing Body Wash is paraben-free.
Phenoxyethanol
Very common preservative — prevents bacterial and fungal growth in products. Found in many of your products. Considered safe at concentrations up to 1%. No skin benefit. Generally well tolerated though some sensitive individuals react to it.
Polysorbate 20 / 60
Emulsifiers and surfactants — help ingredients mix and spread evenly. No skin benefit. Well tolerated.
Sodium Benzoate
Preservative. No skin benefit. Well tolerated in most people. Can form benzene when combined with vitamin C in acidic environments, but concentrations in cosmetics are considered safe by regulatory bodies.
Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS)
Harsh surfactant — strips skin barrier aggressively. Avoid in cleansers for your skin type. Most of your current products wisely avoid it. Not in your current routine but worth knowing to check for when purchasing new cleansers.
Xanthan Gum
Natural thickener and stabilizer derived from fermentation. Gives products a gel-like consistency. No skin benefit. Completely inert and well tolerated.
